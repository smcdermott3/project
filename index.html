The only Hip-Hop review you need

Music, and Hip-Hop has dominated my life since childhood. On this site, you'll be able to find my music reviews and delve deeper into the world of Hip-Hop, and hopefully come out with a bit more knowledge than when you came in.

Kanye West - Devil in a New Dress

Review: As the song begins, the listener is quickly taken in by the angelic vocals in the background. In a few short seconds, West’s lyrics come in to introduce the theme of the song -- that although he may be Holy, the Devil forever tempts him with sin of (in this example) lust. Throughout his verses, his raw emotion is seen in full display as he grapples with the struggle of the dualities. After some time, a welcome but distorted guitar creeps in with a transic solo until the beat swells and Rick Ross’ burley voice fills the songs. Ross offers a braggadocious verse full of excess and offers little in the way of emotion, but for some reason his verse just fits. This is one of Kanye West’s best songs to date, and just an absolute joy to listen to.

Favorite Lyric: You see I always loved that sense of humor / But tonight you should have seen how quiet the room was.

Rating: 9/10

YG - Meet the Flockers

Review: When first listening to YG’s debut album, My Krazy Life, I was immediately brought in by the detail of the song. The album as a whole is a trip through the life of a gang member in South Central Los Angeles, and this song walks the listener to a home invasion by YG and his crew. The lyrics are calculated and cold, yet somehow draws you in and has you rooting for the criminals. Moments of vulnerability, fear even, peak through the macho-ness of the song to allow a glimpse into the psyche of the criminals. This is one of the primary reasons I enjoy Hip-Hop; songs are able to tell stories of topics I have no relation to and therefore I am able to see into others’ lives.

Favorite Lyric: If the police come you gonna find out who your friends now.

Rating: 8/10

Jack Harlow - THRU THE NIGHT

Review: This song is just infectious; it is impossible not to let the beat take over your body and groove to it. The way the two Kentucky natives flow on the beat make their playful voices another instrument that perfectly match the tonality of the song. The song’s content is not too complex -- simply the lyricists perplexed by a girl they met -- but, unless you’re paying attention, this is a fun song to party to.

Favorite Lyric: When I first laid eyes, I was awful enticed.

Rating: 9/10

dvsn - Too Deep

Review: Upon listening to this love song, one realizes that it’s not any normal love song. With the thick bass drowning the minimalist beat, the listener is forced into a trance of tranquility. The soft singing is occasionally punctuated with a choir of voices that fill the song with a fiery passion and gives the song nuanced layers. What is unique about this song, too, is that the lyric contents do not give this song validity, but the atmosphere that is created by assembling the parts (bass, drums, lyrics) is what makes the song stand out.

Favorite Lyric: In too deep.

Rating: 9/10

Action Bronson - Actin Crazy

Review: Action Bronson, a former-gourmet-chef-turned-rapper, is very niche and hard to get into, but he often treats his songs like his food; crafting each with care and exquisite attention to detail. His pen-game is impeccable over this bouncy beat, with his flow falling in beat perfectly and his rhyme scheme highlighting his skill.

Favorite Lyric: I kiss my mother on the cheek, tell her that I love her.

Rating: 7/10

Manila Grey - Timezones

Review: No time is wasted as the beat kicks in and Neeko (one half of the duo) starts rapping. His infectious flow over the beat slowly makes you head bob until a full on groove takes over. Then the chorus, with the soft pianos over the vocalized beat, comes in and Soliven’s angelic voice takes over as the pieces of this song just fall into place. Very few songs can sound this laidback and hype at the same time, and this song achieves just that; you can play this in the background or blast in your car while going on joyrides with your friends.

Favorite Lyric: My G's wih me so I never ride alone.

Rating: 8/10

Kendrick Lamar - m.A.A.d. city

Review: Coming at the climax of Kendrick Lamar’s concept album, m.A.A.d. city is a haunting look into the gang violence that surrounds Lamar in Compton. It is harsh, abrasive, violent, and jarring to listen to, but the song’s impact makes it one of the most memorable songs of modern Hip-Hop.

Part 1: The deafening bass over an ominous Shepard Tone, paired with Lamar’s whiny voice, creates a terrifying atmosphere that our protagonist is in. The setting is Compton, south of Los Angeles, and it is an unforgiving world that claims lives to gang violence daily. Lamar mentions several allusions to seeing dead bodies, guns, and drugs, that run rampant in Compton.

Part 2: The beat then switches with abrupt static, shocking the viewer. A sped up beat kicks in and the pace intensifies. Then the voice of MC Eiht is heard. MC Eiht is one of the oldest rappers to come out of Compton, and his verse describes how little has changed in Compton from his day. However, instead of terrified like Lamar, MC Eiht embraces this lifestyle and thrives off of the violence. His confidence is felt in his tone and the listener understands that those who cannot escape Compton become what they so feared. The cycle continues. Lamar delivers one last verse filled with crime, but he is not seemingly okay with that, and concludes that Compton has shaped him into an imperfect, deranged angel of death.

Favorite Lyric: That was back when I was nine, Joey packed the 9 / Pakistan on every porch is fine, we adapt to crime / Pack a van with four guns at a time / with the sliding doors.

Rating: 10/10
